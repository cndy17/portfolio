A country hosting the Olympics is a once-in-a-generation event. This project examines three recent host nations — France, Japan, and the UK — analyzing sports budgets vs. medal counts, stock market reactions around bid announcements, tourism impacts, GDP per capita effects, and long-term debt accumulation.

## Key findings

**Larger sports investments boost host country performance.** Japan's medals peaked at Tokyo 2021 after a decade of rising budgets. France won 64 medals at Paris 2024 following a sharp increase in sports expenditure.

**Markets react positively to bid announcements, but it's speculative.** Stock prices trend upward in the 30 days after a host city is announced. The effect is short-lived.

**GDP impact is inconsistent; debt increase is not.** The UK and France saw GDP per capita increases post-Games. Japan and Brazil saw minimal impact. Debt as % of GDP rose consistently across every host nation.

## Methods

Data sourced from: Yahoo Finance (stock market), World Bank and IMF (debt and inequality), Bloomberg (retail sales), local tourism databases for France and Japan, Wikipedia and Google for medal counts.

## Conclusion

Is the glory of a few weeks worth the possible systematic financial hampering? The data shows nations commit the resources, markets cheer, athletes perform — but the debt outlasts the celebration. Buyer beware.
