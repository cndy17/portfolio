Exploratory analysis of NYC public high school data to identify which factors — funding, demographics, borough, student-teacher ratios — most strongly correlate with academic outcomes. Uses publicly available NYC DOE datasets.

## Findings

Funding per student is a weaker predictor than student-teacher ratio and chronic absenteeism. Borough differences exist but are largely explained by underlying socioeconomic variables. Schools with strong extracurricular programs consistently outperform on metrics beyond test scores.
